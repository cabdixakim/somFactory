The "u" in uPVC stands for "unplasticized" and uPVC is also called rigid plastic because it doesn't have these additional equipment. UPVC is used more commonly for water pipes than PVC in most of the world except the US. ... CPVC, or chlorinated polyvinyl chloride, performs the purpose

PVC

PVC, or polyvinyl chloride, is a type of plumbing pipe in the drain or vent line. PVC first became common because it was lighter and easier to work with than traditional galvanized steel pipe. PVC pipe is moderately easy to install, requiring little more to be cut than a hacksaw and a miter box. PVC glues with solvents

Rigid cooper pipe

Rigid copper is often used domestically for water supply lines. Rigid copper can be cut quickly with a hacksaw or a special copper tube cutter. The connection is a different matter, as soldering copper pipe together requires a practiced hand. Rigid copper pipe is ideal for providing water, as it does not pose any health risks.

acrylonitrile butadiene styrene(ABS)

The ABS (acrylonitrile butadiene styrene) pipe is used primarily as a drain and vent line. ABS pipe looks very similar to a PVC pipe, except it's black and slightly lighter.

Flexible cooper pipe

Flexible copper pipe or tubing to water heaters, refrigerators, and some sinks are used for the final runs. Flexible copper is only used for small intervals, and can be quickly cut with a hacksaw. The fit around corners can be bent.
